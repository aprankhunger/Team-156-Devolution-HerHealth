What is PCOD?

Polycystic Ovarian Disease (PCOD) is a lifestyle disorder. It is a hormonal disorder in which the ovaries produce excessive androgens (male hormones), leading to irregular menstrual cycles, ovarian cysts, weight gain, acne, and excessive hair growth. It occurs due to a combination of genetic, hormonal, and lifestyle factors, often linked to insulin resistance and inflammation. Poor diet, stress, and lack of exercise can worsen the condition. Managing PCOD requires a balanced diet, regular exercise, and stress control to regulate hormones and restore menstrual health.

Recommended Exercises

For Mild PCOD : Engage in cardio (30-40 min/day) like walking or cycling to boost metabolism, and strength training (3-4 times/week) with squats and planks to build muscle. Yoga (daily), including Butterfly and Cobra Pose, reduces stress and balances hormones, while HIIT (2-3 times/week) with jump squats improves insulin sensitivity. A recommended routine includes morning walks + yoga and evening strength training or HIIT on alternate days for overall well-being.
For Modrate PCOD : cardio (4-5x per week, 30-45 min) like walking or cycling to boost metabolism and strength training (3-4x per week, 30 min) with squats and planks to build muscle. Yoga & stretching (daily, 15-30 min), including Butterfly and Cobra Pose, help balance hormones, while HIIT (optional, 3x per week, 15-20 min) with jump squats enhances insulin sensitivity. Key tips include consistency (4-5 days/week), avoiding over-exercising, and mixing cardio, strength, and yoga for optimal results.
For Severe PCOD : a low-impact cardio routine (5x per week, 30-45 min) like walking, cycling, or Pilates boosts metabolism, while strength training (3x per week, 20-30 min) with glute bridges and leg lifts improves muscle tone. Yoga & stretching (daily, 20-30 min), including Butterfly and Cobra Pose, help balance hormones, and breathwork & meditation (daily, 10-15 min) aid in stress reduction. Key tips include consistent but gentle exercise (4-5 days/week), avoiding intense training, managing stress through yoga, and pairing workouts with a balanced diet for optimal results.
